Hamid Kamangir, PhD

Researcher and ML engineer operating at the intersection of Earth system science, agriculture, climate, and production AI, bridging rigorous scientific modeling and the engineering practices required to ship reliable ML systems at scale.

My work advances AI-enabled geospatial and agroecosystem intelligence. I build hybrid AI–physics frameworks that fuse satellite, airborne, and ground-sensor data using deep learning architectures (including Vision Transformers and multimodal models) to model crop dynamics, severe weather forecasting, and climate impacts on crop yield, and develop explainable, uncertainty-aware models trusted by scientific and operational stakeholders alike. My research appears in venues including CVPR and IEEE Remote Sensing journals, with models like CMAViT and FogNet translating physical domain knowledge into learnable representations.

On the AI engineering side, I have 8+ years designing and operating production ML systems, from embedding-based retrieval and real-time ranking pipelines to large-scale forecasting and decision systems under uncertainty (Microsoft). I work across the full ML stack: LLMs, VLMs, fine-tuning, RAG systems, LLM evaluation, and conversational AI, as well as foundational infrastructure, low-latency inference APIs, feature stores, distributed training, and A/B experimentation frameworks.
