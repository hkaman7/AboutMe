About Hamid Kamangir

Hamid Kamangir is a Postdoctoral Scholar in the Plant AI and Biophysics Lab at UC Davis. He earned a Ph.D. in Agricultural and Environmental Engineering focused on AI-enabled agroecosystem modeling, along with M.Sc. degrees in Computer Science for AI-driven severe weather prediction and Remote Sensing for satellite image analysis.

His research advances AI-enabled geospatial modeling to understand agroecosystem dynamics in a changing climate. Current priorities include:

  • Developing hybrid AI–physics frameworks for agroecosystem simulation to better represent interactions among carbon, water, and nutrient cycles under human and climate pressures.
  • Building integrative data–model learning systems that fuse satellite observations, ground measurements, and model simulations using advanced AI architectures to quantify soil health, greenhouse gas emissions, and water balance dynamics.
  • Advancing multi-sensor geospatial analytics to combine hyperspectral, airborne, and satellite imagery with deep learning, capturing fine-scale spatial heterogeneity and temporal evolution in agricultural and environmental systems.
